THE RIGHT TO INFORMATION ACT, 2005
(Act No. 22 of 2005)
[Received the assent of the President on 15th June 2005]

An Act to provide for setting out the practical regime of right to information for citizens to secure access to information under the control of public authorities, in order to promote transparency and accountability in the working of every public authority.

CHAPTER I – PRELIMINARY

Section 1: Short Title, Extent, and Commencement
This Act may be called the Right to Information Act, 2005. It extends to the whole of India except the State of Jammu and Kashmir (now applicable to J&K as well after reorganization in 2019).

Section 2: Definitions
(a) "Appropriate Government" means the Central Government or the State Government.
(b) "Central Information Commission" means the Central Information Commission constituted under Section 12.
(c) "Central Public Information Officer" means the Central Public Information Officer designated under Section 5(1).
(d) "Information" means any material in any form, including records, documents, memos, e-mails, opinions, advices, press releases, circulars, orders, logbooks, contracts, reports, papers, samples, models, data material held in any electronic form. It also includes information relating to any private body which can be accessed by a public authority under any other law.
(e) "Public authority" means any authority or body or institution of self-government established or constituted by or under the Constitution or by any other law made by Parliament or State Legislature or by notification issued or order made by the appropriate Government. It includes any body owned, controlled or substantially financed, or non-Government organisation substantially financed by the appropriate Government.
(f) "Record" includes any document, manuscript, or file, any microfilm, microfiche, or facsimile copy of a document, any reproduction of image or images embodied in such microfilm, and any other material produced by a computer or any other device.
(g) "Right to Information" means the right to information accessible under this Act which is held by or under the control of any public authority. It includes the right to:
   (i) Inspection of work, documents, records
   (ii) Taking notes, extracts, or certified copies of documents or records
   (iii) Taking certified samples of material
   (iv) Obtaining information in the form of diskettes, floppies, tapes, video cassettes, or in any other electronic mode or through printouts

CHAPTER II – RIGHT TO INFORMATION AND OBLIGATIONS OF PUBLIC AUTHORITIES

Section 3: Right to Information
Subject to the provisions of this Act, all citizens shall have the right to information.

Section 4: Obligations of Public Authorities
(1) Every public authority shall:
(a) Maintain all its records duly catalogued and indexed
(b) Publish within 120 days from the enactment:
   (i) Particulars of its organisation, functions, and duties
   (ii) Powers and duties of its officers and employees
   (iii) Procedure followed in the decision-making process
   (iv) Norms set for the discharge of its functions
   (v) Rules, regulations, instructions, manuals, and records used by employees
   (vi) Categories of documents held
   (vii) Particulars of arrangements for consultation with or representation by the members of the public
   (viii) Boards, councils, committees, and other bodies constituted as part of the authority
   (ix) Directory of its officers and employees
   (x) Monthly remuneration received by each officer and employee
   (xi) Budget allocated to each agency
   (xii) Manner of execution of subsidy programmes
   (xiii) Particulars of recipients of concessions, permits, or authorisations granted
   (xiv) Details in respect of information available in electronic form
   (xv) Particulars of facilities available to citizens for obtaining information
   (xvi) Names, designations, and other particulars of Public Information Officers
   (xvii) Such other information as may be prescribed

Section 5: Designation of Public Information Officers
(1) Every public authority shall designate a Central Public Information Officer or State Public Information Officer for providing information to persons requesting it.
(2) Every public authority shall designate an Assistant Public Information Officer at each sub-divisional level or other sub-district level.

Section 6: Request for Obtaining Information
(1) A person who desires to obtain any information shall make a request in writing or through electronic means in English or Hindi or in the official language of the area, along with prescribed fees, to the Public Information Officer.
(2) An applicant making request for information shall not be required to give any reason for requesting the information.
(3) Where a request cannot be made in writing, the Public Information Officer shall render all reasonable assistance.

Section 7: Disposal of Request
(1) The Public Information Officer shall provide information within 30 days of the receipt of the request.
(2) If the information relates to the life or liberty of a person, it shall be provided within 48 hours.
(3) Where the information is to be provided within 48 hours, the fee shall be waived.
(5) If information is not provided within the specified period, it shall be deemed refusal.

Section 8: Exemptions from Disclosure of Information
(1) The following information is exempt from disclosure:
(a) Information which would prejudicially affect the sovereignty and integrity of India, the security, strategic, scientific, or economic interests of the State, relation with foreign State or lead to incitement of an offence
(b) Information which has been expressly forbidden to be published by any court of law or tribunal
(c) Information the disclosure of which would cause a breach of privilege of Parliament or the State Legislature
(d) Information including commercial confidence, trade secrets, or intellectual property
(e) Information available to a person in his fiduciary relationship
(f) Information received in confidence from foreign Government
(g) Information the disclosure of which would endanger the life or physical safety of any person
(h) Information which would impede the process of investigation or prosecution of offenders
(i) Cabinet papers including records of deliberations of the Council of Ministers, Secretaries and other officers (decisions and reasons shall be made public after decision is taken)
(j) Information which relates to personal information and has no relationship to any public activity or interest (unless larger public interest justifies disclosure)

Section 9: Grounds for Rejection
(1) A request for information may be rejected if it involves an infringement of copyright of any person other than the State.

Section 10: Severability
Where a request for access to information is rejected on the ground that it is in relation to information which is exempt from disclosure, access may be provided to that part of the record which does not contain any information which is exempt from disclosure.

CHAPTER III – THE CENTRAL INFORMATION COMMISSION

Section 12: Constitution of Central Information Commission
(1) The Central Government shall constitute a body called the Central Information Commission.
(2) The Central Information Commission shall consist of:
(a) The Chief Information Commissioner
(b) Such number of Central Information Commissioners not exceeding ten as may be deemed necessary

Section 13: Term of Office and Conditions of Service
The Chief Information Commissioner shall hold office for a term of five years from the date of entering upon his office and shall not be eligible for reappointment.
Amendment 2019: Term and conditions now prescribed by the Central Government.

Section 15: Constitution of State Information Commission
Each State Government shall constitute a State Information Commission.

CHAPTER IV – POWERS AND FUNCTIONS

Section 18: Powers and Functions of Information Commissions
(1) The Central Information Commission or State Information Commission shall receive and inquire into a complaint from any person:
(a) Who has been unable to submit a request to a Public Information Officer
(b) Who has been refused access to any information requested
(c) Who has not been given a response within the time limit specified
(d) Who has been required to pay an amount of fee which he or she considers unreasonable
(e) Who believes that incorrect, incomplete, misleading, or distorted information has been given

Section 19: Appeal
(1) First appeal to an officer senior to the Public Information Officer within 30 days
(2) Second appeal to the Central Information Commission or State Information Commission within 90 days
(3) The burden of proving that a denial of a request was justified shall be on the Public Information Officer

Section 20: Penalties
(1) If the Central Information Commission or State Information Commission is of the opinion that the PIO has without reasonable cause:
- Refused to receive an application for information
- Not furnished information within the time specified
- Malafidely denied the request for information
- Knowingly given incorrect, incomplete, or misleading information
- Destroyed information which was the subject of the request
- Obstructed in any manner in furnishing the information
The Commission shall impose a penalty of Rs. 250 per day until application is received or information is furnished, subject to a maximum of Rs. 25,000.
(2) The Commission shall also recommend disciplinary action against the PIO.

FEES AND CHARGES:
1. Application Fee: Rs. 10 (for Central Government departments)
2. Per page of information: Rs. 2 per page (A4 or A3)
3. For larger size paper: actual cost
4. For inspection of records: no fee for the first hour; Rs. 5 for each subsequent hour
5. For information in electronic form: Rs. 50 per CD/DVD
6. BPL (Below Poverty Line) applicants: no fee required (must provide proof)

WHO CAN FILE AN RTI REQUEST:
1. Any citizen of India (non-citizens cannot use RTI)
2. Can be filed by any person, regardless of age, gender, caste, or occupation
3. No need to give any reason for requesting information
4. Can be filed in English, Hindi, or the official language of the concerned area
5. Can be filed online through the RTI Online Portal (rtionline.gov.in) for Central Government departments

KEY STATISTICS AND IMPACT:
- Over 60 lakh RTI applications are filed every year across India
- RTI has been instrumental in exposing corruption, ensuring transparency
- Several landmark cases have used RTI to bring public accountability
- RTI has been recognized as a fundamental right under Article 19 of the Indian Constitution by the Supreme Court
