PROTECTION OF CHILDREN FROM SEXUAL OFFENCES (POCSO) ACT, 2012
(As amended in 2019)

CHAPTER I – PRELIMINARY

Section 1: Short Title, Extent, and Commencement
This Act may be called the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012. It extends to the whole of India. It came into force on 14th November 2012.

Section 2: Definitions
(a) "Child" means any person below the age of eighteen years.
(b) "Special Court" means a court designated under section 28 for the trial of offences under this Act.
(c) "Shared household" means a household where the person aggrieved lives or has lived in a domestic relationship.

CHAPTER II – SEXUAL OFFENCES AGAINST CHILDREN

Section 3: Penetrative Sexual Assault
A person is said to commit penetrative sexual assault if he commits penetrative sexual assault on a child. This includes sexual intercourse, insertion of objects, and other forms of penetration.

Section 4: Punishment for Penetrative Sexual Assault
Whoever commits penetrative sexual assault shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which shall not be less than seven years but which may extend to imprisonment for life, and shall also be liable to fine.
Amendment 2019: Minimum punishment increased to 10 years, extendable to imprisonment for life.

Section 5: Aggravated Penetrative Sexual Assault
Penetrative sexual assault is deemed aggravated when committed by:
(a) A police officer within the limits of their jurisdiction
(b) A member of the armed forces
(c) A public servant
(d) Staff of a jail, remand home, protection home, observation home, or other place of custody
(e) Staff of a hospital, educational institution, or religious institution
(f) A relative of the child through blood or adoption or marriage or guardianship
(g) A person in a position of trust or authority over the child
(h) Gang penetrative sexual assault on a child
(i) Using deadly weapons, fire, heated substance, or corrosive substance
(j) Causing grievous hurt, bodily harm, or injury to the sexual organs
(k) Making the child pregnant
(l) Inflicting the child with HIV or any other life-threatening disease
(m) Committing penetrative sexual assault more than once
(n) Committing penetrative sexual assault on a child below twelve years of age
(o) A person being relative of the child through blood, adoption, marriage, guardianship, or in foster care or having domestic relationship with a parent of the child or who is living in the same or shared household with the child

Section 6: Punishment for Aggravated Penetrative Sexual Assault
Whoever commits aggravated penetrative sexual assault shall be punished with rigorous imprisonment for a term which shall not be less than twenty years but which may extend to imprisonment for life (which shall mean imprisonment for the remainder of natural life of that person) and shall also be liable to fine, or with death.
Amendment 2019: Death penalty added for aggravated penetrative sexual assault on children below 12 years.

Section 7: Sexual Assault
Whoever, with sexual intent, touches the vagina, penis, anus, or breast of the child or makes the child touch the vagina, penis, anus, or breast of such person or any other person, or does any other act with sexual intent which involves physical contact without penetration, is said to commit sexual assault.

Section 8: Punishment for Sexual Assault
Whoever commits sexual assault shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which shall not be less than three years but which may extend to five years and shall also be liable to fine.

Section 9: Aggravated Sexual Assault
Sexual assault is deemed aggravated under similar circumstances as described under Section 5 for penetrative sexual assault.

Section 10: Punishment for Aggravated Sexual Assault
Whoever commits aggravated sexual assault shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which shall not be less than five years but which may extend to seven years, and shall also be liable to fine.

Section 11: Sexual Harassment
A person is said to commit sexual harassment upon a child when such person with sexual intent:
(i) Utters any word or makes any sound, or makes any gesture or exhibits any object or part of body with the intention that such word or sound shall be heard, or such gesture or object or part of body shall be seen by the child
(ii) Makes a child exhibit his body or any part of his body so as it is seen by such person or any other person
(iii) Shows any object to a child in any form or media for pornographic purposes
(iv) Repeatedly or constantly follows or watches or contacts a child either directly or through electronic, digital or any other means
(v) Threatens to use, in any form of media, a real or fabricated depiction through electronic, film, or digital or any other mode, of any part of the body of the child
(vi) Entices a child for pornographic purposes or gives gratification therefor
(vii) Any word or act intended to cause sexual harassment

Section 12: Punishment for Sexual Harassment
Whoever commits sexual harassment upon a child shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to three years and shall also be liable to fine.

CHAPTER III – USING CHILD FOR PORNOGRAPHIC PURPOSES

Section 13: Use of Child for Pornographic Purposes
Whoever uses a child in any form of media (including electronic, film, internet, or any other mode) for the purposes of sexual gratification shall be guilty of using a child for pornographic purposes.

Section 14: Punishment for Using Child for Pornographic Purposes
(1) First offence: imprisonment not less than five years, fine
(2) Subsequent offence: imprisonment not less than seven years, fine

Section 15: Punishment for Storage of Pornographic Material Involving Child
(1) Whoever stores for commercial purposes pornographic material involving a child: imprisonment up to 3 years, or fine, or both
(2) Whoever stores for purposes of transmitting/distributing/displaying: imprisonment up to 3 years, or fine, or both
(3) Whoever stores with intent to share but hasn't shared: fine not less than 5,000 rupees, and in subsequent offence, not less than 10,000 rupees

CHAPTER IV – ABETMENT AND ATTEMPT

Section 16: Abetment of an Offence
A person abets an offence when he instigates any person to do that offence, engages with one or more persons in any conspiracy for the doing of that offence, or intentionally aids by any act or illegal omission.

Section 17: Punishment for Abetment
Whoever abets any offence under this Act shall be punished with the punishment provided for that offence.

Section 18: Punishment for Attempt to Commit an Offence
Whoever attempts to commit any offence punishable under this Act shall be punished with imprisonment of any description provided for the offence, for a term which may extend to one-half of the imprisonment for life or the longest term of imprisonment.

CHAPTER V – PROCEDURE FOR REPORTING

Section 19: Reporting of Offences
(1) Any person who has apprehension that an offence under this Act is likely to be committed or has knowledge that such an offence has been committed, shall provide such information to the Special Juvenile Police Unit or the local police.
(2) Every report shall be ascribed an entry number and shall be read over to the informant.
(3) Where such report is given by a child, it shall be recorded in simple language so that the child understands.
(4) Where the Special Juvenile Police Unit or local police is satisfied that the child against whom an offence has been committed is in need of care and protection, it shall make immediate arrangement for emergency medical treatment.

Section 20: Obligation of Media, Studio, and Photography Facility
Any media or studio or photographic facility shall not publish or disclose the identity of the child.

Section 21: Punishment for False Complaint or False Information
(1) Whoever makes false complaint or provides false information: imprisonment up to six months, or fine, or both
(2) However, no action against a child who makes false complaint

CHAPTER VI – PROCEDURES FOR RECORDING STATEMENT

Section 24: Recording of Statement of the Child
The statement of the child shall be recorded at the residence of the child or at a place where the child usually resides or a place of his choice and as far as practicable by a woman police officer not below the rank of sub-inspector.

Section 25: Recording of Statement by Magistrate
If the statement is to be recorded by a Magistrate, the Magistrate shall record the statement as spoken by the child in the presence of the parents or any person in whom the child has trust or confidence.

Section 26: Additional Provisions Regarding Statement
(1) The child shall not be called repeatedly to testify
(2) No child shall be detained in the police station in the night

CHAPTER VII – SPECIAL COURTS

Section 28: Designation of Special Courts
The State Government shall designate for each district a Court of Session to be a Special Court for the purpose of this Act.

Section 33: Procedure and Powers of Special Court
(1) A Special Court may take cognizance of any offence without the accused being committed to it for trial
(2) The Special Court shall ensure that the child is not exposed to the accused at the time of recording evidence
(3) The Special Court shall create a child-friendly atmosphere by allowing a family member, guardian, or any person in whom the child has trust to be present

Section 35: Period for Recording Evidence
The evidence of the child shall be recorded within a period of thirty days of the Special Court taking cognizance of the offence and reasons for delay, if any, shall be recorded by the Special Court.

Section 36: Child Not to See Accused
The Special Court shall ensure that the child is not exposed to the accused at the time of recording of the evidence while at the same time ensuring that the accused is in a position to hear the statement of the child.

CHAPTER VIII – MISCELLANEOUS

Section 42A: Act Not in Derogation of Any Other Law
The provisions of this Act shall be in addition to and not in derogation of the provisions of any other law for the time being in force.

Section 44: Monitoring of Implementation
(1) The National Commission for Protection of Child Rights and State Commissions shall monitor the implementation of this Act.
(2) The National Commission or State Commission shall in its annual report include a separate section on the monitoring of the implementation of this Act.

KEY FEATURES AND PRINCIPLES:
1. Gender-neutral: The Act protects children of all genders
2. Mandatory reporting: Any person who knows of an offence must report it
3. Child-friendly procedures: Special provisions for recording evidence
4. Presumption of guilt: The burden of proof lies on the accused
5. Speedy trial: Trial to be completed within one year
6. No time limitation: No limitation period for filing cases
7. Confidentiality: Identity of the child must be protected
8. Support person: Child has right to assistance of a support person

Source: The Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012 (Act No. 32 of 2012), as amended by The Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (Amendment) Act, 2019 (Act No. 25 of 2019). Published in the Gazette of India.
