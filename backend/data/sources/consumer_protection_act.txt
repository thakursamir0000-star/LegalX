THE CONSUMER PROTECTION ACT, 2019
(Act No. 35 of 2019)
[Received the assent of the President on 9th August 2019]

An Act to provide for protection of the interests of consumers and for the purpose of establishment of consumer disputes redressal commissions and consumer mediation cells and for matters connected therewith or incidental thereto.

CHAPTER I – PRELIMINARY

Section 1: Short Title, Extent, Commencement
This Act may be called the Consumer Protection Act, 2019. It extends to the whole of India except the State of Jammu and Kashmir. It replaces the Consumer Protection Act, 1986.

Section 2: Definitions
(1) "Consumer" means any person who buys any goods or hires or avails of any service for a consideration which has been paid or promised or partly paid and partly promised, or under any system of deferred payment. It does not include a person who obtains goods for resale or for any commercial purpose.
(2) "Complaint" means any allegation in writing made by a complainant regarding unfair trade practice, defective goods, deficiency in service, excessive price, hazardous goods/services, or misleading advertisement.
(3) "Defect" means any fault, imperfection, or shortcoming in the quality, quantity, potency, purity, or standard which is required to be maintained under any law.
(4) "Deficiency" means any fault, imperfection, shortcoming, or inadequacy in the quality, nature, and manner of performance which is required to be maintained under any law.
(5) "E-commerce" means buying or selling of goods or services including digital products over digital or electronic network.
(6) "Misleading advertisement" means any advertisement which falsely describes a product or service, gives false guarantee, or is likely to mislead the consumers.
(7) "Product liability" means the responsibility of a product manufacturer or product seller to compensate for any harm caused to a consumer by such defective product.
(8) "Unfair contract" means a contract between a manufacturer/trader/service provider and a consumer having such terms which cause significant change in the rights of the consumer.

CHAPTER II – CONSUMER RIGHTS

Section 2(9): Rights of Consumers
The Consumer Protection Act, 2019 provides the following rights to consumers:
(a) Right to be protected against marketing of goods, products, or services which are hazardous to life and property
(b) Right to be informed about the quality, quantity, potency, purity, standard and price of goods, products or services
(c) Right to be assured, wherever possible, access to a variety of goods, products, or services at competitive prices
(d) Right to be heard and to be assured that consumer interests will receive due consideration at appropriate fora
(e) Right to seek redressal against unfair trade practice or restrictive trade practices or unscrupulous exploitation of consumers
(f) Right to consumer awareness

CHAPTER III – CENTRAL CONSUMER PROTECTION AUTHORITY (CCPA)

Section 10: Establishment of CCPA
The Central Government shall establish a body known as the Central Consumer Protection Authority (CCPA) to regulate matters relating to violation of consumer rights, unfair trade practices, and misleading advertisements.

Section 18: Powers and Functions of CCPA
(a) Protect, promote, and enforce the rights of consumers
(b) Prevent violation of consumer rights
(c) Prevention of unfair trade practices
(d) Ensure no false or misleading advertisement is made
(e) Ensure no person takes part in publication of misleading advertisement
(f) Inquire or investigate into violations of consumer rights
(g) Intervene in any proceedings before the District Commission, State Commission, or National Commission
(h) Review matters relating to factors inhibiting enjoyment of consumer rights
(i) Recommend adoption of best international practices
(j) Recommend to Central/State Government such institutional changes as may be necessary

Section 21: Power to Issue Directions Against False or Misleading Advertisements
CCPA may issue directions to the trader, manufacturer, endorser, advertiser, or publisher to:
(a) Discontinue such advertisement
(b) Modify the advertisement
(c) Impose penalty up to Rs. 10 lakhs (extendable to Rs. 50 lakhs for subsequent offence)
(d) Ban endorser from endorsing for up to 1 year (extendable to 3 years for subsequent offence)

CHAPTER IV – CONSUMER DISPUTES REDRESSAL COMMISSION

Section 28: District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (District Commission)
Established in each district. Composition: President (judge) + not less than 2 members.

Section 34: Jurisdiction of District Commission
Complaints where value of goods or services does not exceed Rs. 1 crore.

Section 47: State Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (State Commission)
Established by each State Government. Handles complaints where value exceeds Rs. 1 crore but does not exceed Rs. 10 crores. Also hears appeals from District Commissions.

Section 53: National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (National Commission)
Handles complaints where value exceeds Rs. 10 crores. Hears appeals from State Commissions.

Section 35: Manner of Filing Complaint
(a) A complaint may be filed by a consumer, any recognized consumer association, the Central Government, State Government, or Central Authority
(b) Complaint may be filed electronically
(c) Complaint shall be filed within 2 years from the date on which the cause of action has arisen

CHAPTER V – MEDIATION

Section 74: Consumer Mediation Cell
Consumer mediation cells shall be established and attached to each District Commission, State Commission, and the National Commission.

Section 75: Empanelment of Mediators
The State Government shall prepare a panel of mediators for each District Commission and State Commission.

Section 80: Settlement Through Mediation
Where a settlement is reached through mediation, the same shall be reduced to writing, signed by the parties, and placed before the Commission for recording.

CHAPTER VI – PRODUCT LIABILITY

Section 82: Product Liability Action
A product liability action may be brought by a complainant against a product manufacturer, product service provider, or product seller.

Section 83: Liability of Product Manufacturer
The product manufacturer shall be liable in a product liability action if:
(a) The product contains a manufacturing defect
(b) The product is defective in design
(c) There is a deviation from manufacturing specifications
(d) The product does not conform to the express warranty
(e) The product fails to contain adequate instructions for correct usage

Section 84: Liability of Product Service Provider
The product service provider shall be liable if the service provided was faulty or imperfect or deficient or inadequate.

Section 85: Liability of Product Seller
The product seller shall be liable if:
(a) The seller has exercised substantial control over the designing, testing, manufacturing, packaging, or labeling of a product
(b) The seller has altered or modified the product
(c) The seller has made an express warranty of a product independent of the manufacturer

CHAPTER VII – OFFENCES AND PENALTIES

Section 88: Punishment for Manufacture or Sale of Adulterated Goods
(1) Not causing injury: imprisonment up to 6 months and fine up to Rs. 1 lakh
(2) Causing injury not amounting to grievous hurt: imprisonment up to 1 year and fine up to Rs. 3 lakhs
(3) Causing injury amounting to grievous hurt: imprisonment up to 7 years and fine up to Rs. 5 lakhs
(4) Causing death: imprisonment not less than 7 years extendable to imprisonment for life and fine not less than Rs. 10 lakhs

Section 89: Punishment for Manufacture or Sale of Spurious Goods
(1) Not causing injury: imprisonment up to 1 year and fine up to Rs. 3 lakhs
(2) Causing injury not amounting to grievous hurt: imprisonment up to 3 years and fine up to Rs. 5 lakhs
(3) Causing injury amounting to grievous hurt: imprisonment up to 7 years and fine up to Rs. 7 lakhs
(4) Causing death: imprisonment for life and fine not less than Rs. 10 lakhs

UNFAIR TRADE PRACTICES (Section 2(47)):
- Making false or misleading representation about goods/services
- Permitting publication of advertisements which are misleading
- Offering gifts, prizes, contests to lure consumers
- Not issuing bill or receipt for goods sold
- Refusing to take back defective goods or to withdraw deficient services
- Manufacturing spurious goods or offering such goods for sale
- Not issuing bill or cash memo for goods/services
- Hoarding or destroying goods to raise cost
- Refusing after-sales service or spare parts
- Making disclosure of personal information given by the consumer in confidence unless required by law

E-COMMERCE RULES (Consumer Protection (E-Commerce) Rules, 2020):
1. Every e-commerce entity shall provide information about return, refund, exchange, warranty, and guarantee
2. No e-commerce entity shall adopt any unfair trade practice whether in the course of business or otherwise
3. Every e-commerce entity shall provide a complaint redressal mechanism
4. Sellers on marketplace must provide pre-purchase information about goods/services
5. E-commerce entities shall appoint a grievance officer and display contact details on their platform

Source: The Consumer Protection Act, 2019 (Act No. 35 of 2019), published in the Gazette of India. Consumer Protection (E-Commerce) Rules, 2020.
